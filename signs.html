Phase 2

I made two stamp models and created CAM toolpaths for each of the two stamp models. The first one is the letter Y which is my initial of first name. The font I choose to design in Illustrator is Minion Variable Concept. I like this elegant pen edge. I also designed a symbol that was my animated avatar. In the beginning, I made a mistake, since I added the detail of eyelashes and highlight, then all the model bodies did not combine together, although the CAM Toolpaths could be work in stimulation. As I subtracted the details of the eyes and merged all the body models, the CAM could produce Toolpath better on my stamp model.

Final Sign Project Stamp

For my final documentation, I decided to make these two signs for Las Gatos Creek County Park and my neighborhood area. I designed a sign for Las Gatos Creek County Park. I noticed that there needs to be a sign here to remind people that there is no road ahead. For the "Out of Town" sign, I found that the road was full of potholes, specifically on rainy days that there were puddles everywhere, giving me the feeling that I was in the country rather than the city. The sign emphasizes the importance of better urban planning and people's lack of responsibility for the intersections in front of their homes, and their contributions to community development.

Final Stamp

I wanted to simplify my avatar graphic for the stamp by removing some eye details for the final stamp. Since the original avatar graphic is created by coding, I think it would be fun to make a woodcut and print it. To paint on the wood surface, I used India ink and a painted brush. Initially, I painted the surface of the need to print, but as the friction increased, ink penetration into the wood became easier, so I chose to paint the whole wood black to make the overall look tidy. I standing on a block of wood for 20 to 30 seconds, putting pressure on this print to make the color more intense and complete.
