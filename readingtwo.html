Q1. What are the differences between site specific art work and the idea of art interventions.

Site-specific art is an artist who explicitly did work for a specific location. The installation of the work, in general, displays the work relate to the environment, and sometimes, it is linked to environmental art.
However, Art Intervention is an interaction with existing artwork, audience, venue/space, or circumstance. Art interventions are often purposeful because artists are trying to change existing situations, such as politics or economics.

Q2. What are the similarities and differences regarding the work of Walter De Maria and Banksy? include your thoughts on how their work might interpret ideas of site specificness and interventions

The similarity between Walter De Maria and Banksy is that both artists use their installation works to aware their audiences and connect the work concepts to the reality of our society.

The difference between them is that Walter De Maria's work is based on a particular location, is Site-specific art. Considering the environment, for example, the loft filled with Earth is located in the city of New York, called The New York Earth Room. However, Bansky has displayed his artwork as an ongoing public exhibit where he thinks appropriate for his purpose, is Art Intervention. For example, the Street Art Exhibition, selling his work on the roadside, called "Better Out Than In."
