Back Story

The population growth and atmospheric pollution are seriously threatening the balance between natural ecosystems. As a result, the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is increasing, and the carbon dioxide "greenhouse effect" directly affects the climate change on the earth's surface. Due to the greenhouse effect, the polar ice melted, and the sea covered everything on the planet. The earth's planet became an ocean world. Humans could only live on the abandoned ships. But with space at a premium, wander people started to look even deeper for somewhere to live better and look for the natural resources for survival. Oceanographers and architects have considered designing laboratories and underwater habitats to allow people to live and work in submerged worlds.
Humans try to adapt to the living environment of the ocean world. Thus, artificial aqualungs were invented. When they were installed in the human body, they could use the artificial aqua lungs for breathing, disintegrating oxygen from the surrounding water, which altered the respiratory system and made it possible for humans to survive underwater. The aqualung is synthesized from a special material. This synthetic material can continuously take in oxygen from air and water without the aid of other means. Humans have been able to live in both ships and the sea since now.
