Moduality, Tessellation & Complexity


Phase 1

First Pass

First, I follow the steps to create this simple Origami pattern, which uses multiple modules to create the Petra star pattern. This is the first time I've created Petra Star, and I think it's interesting and there's a lot of possibilities for innovation. I use four different colors of paper to make Petra stars pattern. Then undo the module to play with it to make another shape. I rearrange them, add folds, create new shapes and arrange them so that they can stand on a plane and form a three-dimensional model. Next, I did my first test based on this simple triangle tessellation video on how to create tessellation shapes in patterns. In the beginning, I designed a wavy edge and a shape, with each edge connected to itself. Then I cut the shape by hand using the cutting method, and I made the shape in 6 pieces. I can rearrange them to create new patterns, and here are the different ways they can be assembled.

Second Pass

My groupmates and I decided to take a simplistic approach in our shared one common side design for the tessellation. Therefore, we choose an equilateral triangle to begin our design pattern. Everyone in our group has designed can be connected on both sides of the unique design. Although some empty spaces were included in our design, we did tesselate well with the one common side to make various shapes.Using the illustrator's pathfinder functions, I create "protruding" and "receding" shapes that share one common side with my group members. My design is more of a smooth curve, and it seems like an abstract plant totem. Because of the use of triangles, I only need to change the two shapes that can be stitched together.

Regrading to my group's approach to making fasteners, each group member made different mediums to create structures in 3d dimensional shapes. I worked with paper, Brian worked with cardboard, and Magen worked with foam. For my design, I made two fastening systems with lots and hooks. I created the two shapes for option A and used thick drawing paper as a medium. The figure 1 pattern was inspired by the Modular Kirigami website, and it has four slots, about 12 cm long. I used a slender design with a certain symmetry to connect the two patterns in the four lots. Because of the drawing paper I use, it's very stretchable and malleable compared to wood; it can twist, the two patterns are twined like a tree vine, and it can stand up and become a three-dimensional structure. I think it will be easy to translate this technique to laser cutting for paper medium. However, my fastener design is only good for paper or other thinner media, not suitable for wood or other hard materials. Figure 2, which started with the equilateral triangle, was inspired by the Puzzle Lamps about 4 cm long; it has three edges the same hook can connect each pattern. Final Design


About our team's approach to the project, we continued to do the game design concept of Mario board gamed, because we were satisfied with the result of using acrylic for our test cut. Thus, we decided to choose acrylic as the material for the final version. After discussion, we listed the game objects we wanted to do to divide the work.
Our board game is designed for normal 2-4 player and can turns it into a board game for 8 players. Players are recommended to be 8 years old or older. We want our work to work with famous game characters, build up on existing designs and innovate a physics game, add our own fasteners and design for the board game. We also designed the base to act as the playground for the game, giving players the freedom to assemble the maps they want to create their own game world.
Our group decide used a promotional board game to attract viewers with fun and colorful objects and a unique, innovative game rule. The first part of the video is introducing the board game. Then, show the assembly process how the characters or base are connected. Next, the video is explained the basic rules of the game and shows the gameplay demo. For example, build a fun structure, roll the giant dice and moving the character. We decided to use the rock version of a Mario song as background music.
I believe our team is well designed in terms of success. The various game objects make the game look very colorful and entertaining while maintaining some of the styles of classic games object and creative game concepts. We have a quite productive work process, everyone works well together, and we can accomplish every task on time.
In some ways, we could have improved. If we had more time, I would suggest adding a narrative to the content of the video, such as listing a relationship map of the characters. In the introduction video, the relationship between the characters and the add narrative story allows the audience to be more involved with it.
